Our Story

Three generations
of honest farming.

In 1987, Harold and Edna Greenwood planted their first seeds on 40 acres of Vermont soil. They believed then — as we believe now — that food grown with care, patience, and respect for the land simply tastes better.

Today, their grandchildren tend the same land, now expanded to 280 acres. We've never used synthetic pesticides. We've never compromised on quality. And we never will.

🌱

Soil-First Philosophy

We build healthy soil through composting, cover crops, and crop rotation — no shortcuts.

💧

Water Stewardship

Drip irrigation and rainwater harvesting reduce our water use by 60% vs. conventional farms.

🐝

Pollinator Sanctuary

20 acres dedicated to wildflower meadows supporting 40+ native bee species.
